David Kaufmann is the Assistant Professor of Spatial Development and Urban Policy at ETH Zürich. He serves as the Director of the Network City and Landscape and as the Deputy Director of the Institute for Spatial and Landscape Development (both at ETH Zürich). He is an urban studies scholar with an interest in planning, policy, and migration studies. His research motivation is to understand and contribute to sustainable, democratic, and just urban development through policy-making and planning. He is the co-editor of the forthcoming book Global Urban Policy (2025, University of Michigan Press), the author of the book Varieties of Capital Cities (2018, Edward Elgar), and the co-author of the book The Political Economy of Capital Cities (2018, Routledge).

Sébastien Lambelet is postdoctoral fellow at York University (EUC Faculty & CITY Institute) since February 2024. He is also Lecturer in the Faculty of Social Sciences at the University of Geneva, where he obtained his PhD in Political Science in late 2019. Overall, Sébastien’s research focuses on urban and metropolitan governance, policy studies, cross-border metropolitan areas, land-use planning, land policies and no net land take policy objectives. Currently, he is conducting fieldwork in Toronto and Ottawa-Gatineau to compare the governance frameworks of these two Canadian city regions to the Swiss cities of Geneva and Zurich he investigated during his PhD.

Xuefei Ren is Professor of Urban Studies at Michigan State University and a Fellow of the Canadian Institute for Advanced Research (CIFAR). Her research explores urban governance and the built environment from a comparative perspective, with expertise spanning China, India, Brazil, and North America. She is the author of three award-winning books, including Governing the Urban in China and India: Land Grabs, Slum Clearance and the War on Air Pollution (Princeton University Press, 2020), which received the Best Book Award in Urban Sociology from the American Sociological Association and an Honorable Mention in Urban Politics from the American Political Science Association. She earned her PhD from the University of Chicago and divides her time between Chicago and Michigan.

Roger Keil is Distinguished Research Professor at the Faculty of Environmental and Urban Change, York University in Toronto and a Fellow of CIFAR’s Humanity’s Urban Future program. He researches global suburbanization, urban political ecology, cities and infectious disease, infrastructure, and regional governance. He is the author of Pandemic Urbanism (Polity, 2023, with S. Harris Ali & Creighton Connolly) and Suburban Planet (Polity, 2018). He has recently edited two volumes on Peripheral Centralities (with Nick Phelps and Paul Maginn, 2025) and previously The Globalizing Cities Reader (Routledge, 2017, with Xuefei Ren), After Suburbia (UTP, 2022 with Fulong Wu) and Turning Up the Heat: Urban political ecology for a climate emergency (MUP, 2023, with Maria Kaika, Tait Mandler & Yannis Tzaninis, 2023).

Zachary Dark is a PhD candidate in Environmental Studies at York University (Toronto, Ontario). His dissertation research examines the role of state-owned hydroelectric utilities in Canadian decarbonization efforts. He is particularly interested in how historical and ongoing colonial dynamics shape the energy landscape in Canada. His dissertation research is supported by a Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council doctoral scholarship.

Alice Guilbert is a PhD researcher and teaching assistant at the Institute for Environmental Science of the University of Geneva. Her thesis, with the co-supervision of Professors Marlyne Sahakian and Armelle Choplin, investigates the topic of thermal comfort and environmental justice. It aims at understanding the socially differentiated impacts of climate change and energy crises on comfort within the homes of residents in the city of Geneva, and includes a study of thermal social practices and public adaptation policies.

Ofelia Freidhof has been part of the Spatial Development and Urban Policy research group at the Institute for Spatial and Landscape Development at ETH Zurich since October 2024. Alongside her role, she is pursuing a master’s degree in Comparative and International Studies at ETH Zurich. Her academic background includes a bachelor's degree in Social Sciences and Economics at the University of Bern and hands-on experience at the Swiss Federal Institute of Aquatic Science and Technology. Her research interests include policy analysis, as well as the design and implementation processes of public policy.

Brennan Standel is a political ecologist and researcher specializing in sustainable land management, climate adaptation, and international development. He holds a master’s degree in Geography from Michigan State University and has conducted fieldwork in Senegal’s Ferlo region, where he investigated the socio-ecological impacts of the Great Green Wall and the governance of pastoralist livelihoods. His work engages critically with themes of environmental justice, restoration, and community participation, drawing on mixed-methods research, geospatial analysis, and ethnographic inquiry. He earned his bachelors at Macalester College, where he was deeply involved in urban agriculture and food justice initiatives. His early work focused on reimagining urban space through community-driven environmental projects.

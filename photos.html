Blacks in Green (BIG) is a Chicago-based, Black-led environmental justice organization dedicated to building sustainable, self-sufficient Black communities through local clean energy, green infrastructure, and economic development. With its flagship Sustainable Square Mile™ model, BIG promotes community-owned solutions that address climate, housing, and racial equity. As a collaborator in the Climate Boomtowns project, BIG offers grounded insight into how grassroots urban initiatives can respond to climate change while empowering historically marginalized neighborhoods.

"BIG is restoring the childhood home of Civil Rights martyr Emmett Till into a museum commemorating the Till Family legacy of courage and forgiveness. Located at the center of our West Woodlawn neighborhood, the museum will tell the story of Emmett Till and Mamie Till-Mobley and their Great Migration family. The restored building will house a rich array of programming, ranging from education to performance, while meeting the highest standards for net-zero energy performance and construction."* — Image and text sourced from the BIG website
